Helpful Tips for Better Cooking

Ingredient Substitutions

Why it's helpful: Increase flexibility when ingredients are missing.

  • Substitute Greek yogurt for sour cream.
  • No buttermilk? Mix 1 cup milk with 1 tbsp lemon juice.

Preparation Tips

Why it's helpful: Saves time and reduces stress.

  • Chop vegetables and measure spices before starting.
  • Marinate proteins the night before.

Cooking Techniques

Why it's helpful: Elevates flavor and texture.

  • Sear meats on high heat for better crust.
  • Fold egg whites gently to keep air in batters.

Storage & Reheating

Why it's helpful: Reduces waste and extends freshness.

  • Refrigerate leftovers for up to 3 days in airtight containers.
  • Reheat in oven at 350°F for best texture.

Make-Ahead Instructions

Why it's helpful: Ideal for planning meals or entertaining.

  • Prepare sauces and dressings 2–3 days in advance.
  • Assemble casseroles ahead and bake before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Why it's helpful: Helps complete the meal and adds appeal.

  • Serve with salad, bread, or rice depending on the dish.
  • Top with fresh herbs or lemon zest for brightness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Why it's helpful: Prevents errors that affect taste or texture.

  • Do not overmix batters—lumps are okay.
  • Preheat oven properly before baking.

Dietary Modifications

Why it's helpful: Makes recipes inclusive for different diets.

  • Use plant milk and vegan cheese for dairy-free versions.
  • Use almond flour for gluten-free baking.

Portion Adjustments

Why it's helpful: Adapt recipes for any number of servings.

  • Double for crowds or freeze leftovers.
  • Halve the recipe for small portions—adjust cooking time as needed.
